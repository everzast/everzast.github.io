Defensive Weapon

Mar 16th, 2015 6:15 am

California does not like you to have weapons. Well, except cars. We have lots and lots of cars trying to take me and my family out. That appears to be legal. All you need is a license to kill, which you get after surviving the grueling world of the DMV.

But other kinds of weapons are not very easy to get or legal to carry. Basically no one gets a carry permit for a firearm unless they are the police, military, or similar. This seems like a good choice, although other states pick differently. And large knives are illegal to carry, which again seems a plausibly reasonable choice even if interfering with Dundee’s outfit. But the stranger one is that any defensive club-like weapon is illegal to carry. An expandable baton seems a pretty reasonable deterrent to a larger/stronger attacker and not really useful offensively in 99%+ of situations. But no, that is illegal. You can not carry a club-like weapon for defense. If you happen to have a kind-of-heavy flashlight nearby, you can use it opportunistically for defense. But you can not carry it around for defense. You must carry it around to shine light on things…

For defense you can carry a stun-gun or mace. I am pretty sure a stun-gun will not stop a zombie. Actually stun-guns are very iffy weapons unless they convert to a direct mode after shooting the darts. We lost a police officer in the area due (reportedly) to a stun-gun failure. But I have never used one, so I need to do that at some point. Mace is also not likely to work on a zombie… they might even like the aroma :-/

So preparing for hand-to-hand with zombies proves to be problematic. I can swing a tennis racket really fast and do carry them around in my car (“no, not for defense: for tennis”), so maybe I just need to make a back holster for them :-)

Could be a new fashion statement.
