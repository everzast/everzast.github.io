Be Careful What You Write About?

Dec 9th, 2014 6:15 am

Apparently I have the ability to cause me (and proximate Koi) to experience the things I am writing about…

I was working on checking out Christmas light strands, and it rained in California recently (See http://SupersonicFox.com/ for how shocking this is), and somehow there was a short. Now in most houses this would trip something and you would just flip a switch, but I live in the http://SlumsOfPaloAlto.com/ in a rented house, so my house is not normal. And it blew out the circuit breaker vs. doing something easily recoverable.

A call to an electrician on a Sunday provided no results, and the circuit that was blown holds several thousand dollars of meat for http://FullOfLifeFood.com.

So I needed to either run a long extension cord, or get me some electricity.

One of my rules is that I don’t buy bad stuff. I don’t work with bad people either (preferring friendly mages or at least friendly competent people). I also never cross-buy: Use one person’s resources to buy from a different person. So I don’t use amazon to buy from a store or a store to buy from amazon. I buy lots of things from Amazon, but however fast they deliver… without the Quad-Copter-Droid system I was not going to get something good from Amazon in the time required to save the meat.

So I went to http://HomeDepot.com and studied the reviews. I saw a lot of generators, but they all weighed 100+ pounds. Yes, I can lift 100+ pounds, but try to avoid it. And I buy things intending that my wife and daughters can use them if they need to. Pretty sure Katrina can’t lift 100 pounds.

But in amongst these behemoths was a diamond: a Ryobi generator that weighs maybe 40 pounds. I have Ryobi equipment and they are very nice. Best battery system I have. So a trip to two Home Depots (stores are bad at tracking inventory) and surviving the nastiest parking lot traffic I have been in recently, and I now have the Ryobi generator. The reviews said to use different oil and a different plug, so I bought the oil but didn’t know the size of the plug without opening the box.

Strangely the box and instructions never mentioned the gas size, but a small spill and a non-spill confirm it is a bit less than a gallon (or my two-gallon gas can is quite a bit more than two gallons).

Very nice generator. Supposedly quiet. If that is true, I hope never to need one of the behemoths!
